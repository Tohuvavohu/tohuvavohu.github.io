Biography

November 7: I will be giving a lunch talk to the Astro department at Penn State on Swift's hunt for EM counterparts to gravitational-wave events.
November 5: I will be giving a talk titled `Searching for the radiative decay of sterile neutrino dark matter with Swift XRT' at the Mid-Atlantic APS in Newark, New Jersey.
October 30: I will be giving a lay-talk to a public audience at Astronomy on Tap: State College titled 'Hearing and Seeing the Universe: Hunting for EM counterparts to gravitational-waves with Swift.'
October 19: I will be giving a talk titled Swift as a follow-up engine for GW triggers: Improving for O3 at the IAU Symposium Gravitational Wave Astrophysics: Early Results from GW Searches and Electromagnetic Counterparts in Baton Rouge, Louisiana.
October 14: I will be giving a talk titled 'The absence of (true) bound states for the single-particle Dirac equation on the Kerr black hole' at the 27th Midwest Relativity Meeting in Ann Arbor, Michigan.

I am currently a member of the Science Operations Team for the NASA Swift Explorer mission based at Penn State. Previously I worked with Wolfram building tools for quantum computing simulations. Before coming to Penn State I was an undergraduate at Reed College where I studied physics and wrote a thesis on quantum mechanics in curved space-times under the supervision of Dr. Joel Franklin. In the summer of 2015 I was supervised by Dr. Kevork Abazajian at the University of California, Irvine where we studied ways to measure the combined neutrino mass using cosmological probes of Large Scale Structure. I have also done research on the limits and applicability of digital simulations of highly chaotic systems. I aspire to be a better writer, and – along with descriptions of past and current projects – you may occasionally find articles I am writing on this site.

In previous lives I worked as a custom frame builder, composite repair technician, mechanic, apprentice shipwright, and climbing guide.
