I am a Vanier Scholar and PhD student in astrophysics at the University of Toronto. My current research focuses on multi-messenger astrophysics (with light, gravitational waves, and occasionally neutrinos), and fast or anomalous transients. Generally I am interested in using high-energy astrophysical phenomena as probes of fundamental physics.
To enable this, we need more data! I focus much of my time on techniques, systems, and design for rapid-response space telescopes in the high-alert-rate transient era.
I'm going to build a new space telescope(s)!

In previous lives I worked as a custom frame builder, composite repair technician, mechanic, apprentice shipwright, and climbing guide.

These lines are commonly translated as: “In the beginning, God created the Heavens and the Earth. And the Earth was Tohu va-vohu and darkness was on the face of the deep…”
